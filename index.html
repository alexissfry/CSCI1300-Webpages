Overview

Although Pastiche truly sells impeccable pastries, their website is difficult to navigate and learn, has accessibility issues, is not responsive, and fails to embody the coziness and European essense of this Federal Hill staple.

Objective

The objective of this redesign is to make Pastiche's corner of the internet embody the experience of walking into their shop‚Äîthe smell of butter and sugar wafting from freshly baked pastries and the intricate painted murals of rolling green hills‚Äîto invite new and loyal customers alike inside. Another goal make sure that no component of the site is outdated and that there is a modern feel throughout, which which will ensure that this 40-year old bakery is able stay relevant in our increasingly digital world.

Scope

This project exclusively focuses on redesigning the homepage of Pastiche's website, which is why some clickable links are just simulated.

Design Process

Early Stage Sketches

The starting, 9-box sketch was an opportunity to ideate and put pen to paper on any landing pages that came to mind for Pastiche! After reflecting on my initial, rough ideas, I gravitated towards a very minimalistic landing page that embodied the European-style of Pastiche and invited users to continue scrolling.

When approaching the final sketch, I knew that I wanted to present photos of not just the desserts Pastiche sells but the store itself and its history early on. Then, I focused on making sure there were clear divisions between information throughout. This step gave me a thorough mental model of what I wanted to accomplish in the low-fidelity wireframes.

Low-fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity wireframes were created using Figma and were essential in the process of refining the design to make sure I was addressing user pain points identified during research and planning out responsiveness across desktop and mobile interfaces. Then, I was able to move onto making all of my ideas come to life in the high-fidelity wireframes and standardizing my entire interface through the style guide.

Style Guide

High-fidelity Prototypes

The high-fidelity prototypes fully integrate the general layout in the low-fidelity, all the components and attributes in the style guide, and aims to embody the spirit of Pastiche, Providence's historic, European-style bakery shop. Certain components of the interface were designed intentionally to accomplish my objective:

  • Title and section header font: Sticking with the original "Pastiche" logo font allows consistentcy throughout the interface and gives a established feel
  • About section image carousel and text: The description alongside images of Pastiche's storefront, bakers, and interior immediately give the users an understanding of the bakery and what it has to offer
  • Favorites section cakes and order button: The floating nature of the cakes allow users to easily view customer favorites and the constrasting, centered order button gives an easy solution for users to satisfy their sweet tooth
  • Complete, scaled footer: The completeness and organization of the footer decreases user confusion and presents information that is easy to understand and interact with

Conclusion

I found this project to be quite challenging and time-intensive given that it was my first time using Bootstrap and media queries! At the same time, the process of redesigning an interface was really rewarding. More specifically, I really enjoyed the process of researching and designing a digital interface from square zero to a relatively functional and responsive final website. I found it especially satisfying because I am often frustrated how I cannot provide direct feedback when interfaces frustrate me. I loved how I could take it into my own hands with this project, and I also learned really useful UX principles through the process of doing and iterating.
